Our Mission:

To promote modern and sustainable agricultural land management practices in order to drive food security and to enhance rural development in accordance with government policy.

Our Vision:

The Agricultural Land Management Division (ALMD) will improve and maintain a database for the sustainable use of soils and agricultural lands.

About ALMD:

The Agricultural Land Management Division (ALMD) is an integral part of the Technical Directorate within the Ministry of Agriculture (MOA). In November 2013, the division was renamed from the Rural Physical Planning Division (RPPD). Its primary objective is to provide expert advisory services on soil and agricultural land use management that aligns with government policies. By working closely with farmers and other stakeholders, the ALMD aims to enhance crop production while preserving and improving the quality of available soil resources. With this approach, the division strives to support sustainable agricultural practices that benefit both the environment and local communities.

The Division will undertake new and expanded programmes aimed at monitoring, investigation and research as well as advisory services to help farmers solve problems associated with low yields in crop production. ALMD Core Functions:

  • Conducting soils and land cover/land use surveys
  • Conducting rural land evaluation assessments and make recommendations on the use of agricultural lands
  • Conducting land capability assessments
  • Assessing soil fertility and make recommendations for soil and land management
  • Conducting soil and soil amendment tests and making fertilizer recommendations to farmers and special interest groups
  • Design and prepare maps
  • GIS data processing, manipulation, analysis and output
  • Capture spatial data through aerial photointerpretation, satellite imagery and global positioning systems
  • Agricultural assessment of subdivision applications
  • Chemical and physical analyses on growth media, chemical analyses on plant tissue and water samples
